Mandate of the Commission

The mandate of the Commission, as specified in the NAICOM Act 1997, includes the following functions:

  • Establish standards for the conduct of insurance business in Nigeria.
  • Approve rates of insurance premiums to be paid for all classes of insurance.
  • Ensure adequate protection of strategic government assets and other properties.
  • Regulate transactions between insurers and reinsurers within and outside Nigeria.
  • Act as an adviser to the Federal Government on all insurance-related matters.
  • Approve standards, conditions, and warranties for all classes of insurance.
  • Protect insurance policyholders, beneficiaries, and third parties in insurance contracts.
  • Publish annual reports and statistics on the insurance industry.
  • Advise government ministries, agencies, and statutory bodies on insurance matters.
  • Support educational programs of the Chartered Insurance Institute of Nigeria and the West African Insurance Institute.
  • Perform other activities related to its functions under the Act.

Our Goals

  • Safeguard policyholders and improve confidence in the industry.
  • Strengthen our supervisory capabilities and organizational effectiveness.
  • Improve the safety and soundness of the Nigerian insurance industry.
  • Foster innovation and sustainability in the Nigerian insurance industry.
  • Enhance overall insurance accessibility and penetration in Nigeria.
